Code of Conduct

Standard Regulations

1.

To join TOG you must be 16 years or older, or be authorized by the board of directors must have a microphone must be willing to contribute to community and Clan members must have their recruiters gamertag in their bio and the date they where recruited and in their location they must have their rank in it at all times.

2.

Members must read and follow the TOG Code of Conduct, policies and the Xbox live terms of service.

3.

Members takes pride in his or her membership and is encouraged but never forced to change their gamertag to reflect TOG unless they are staff sergeant or higher which then they are required to have a name change.

4.

Members must show respect toward all members and non-members, and their opinions, we do not tolerate discrimination in any form this includes but is not limited to racism, sexism or harassment, by or towards, any member of TOG in any situation.

5.

Members must be proactive members in their squad, division, and clan as a whole, including getting recruits, performing rank responsibilities, etc.

6.

All members are entitled to the “Open Door Policy” with any other leader of TOG. However members are expected to follow the “chain of command” and report to leadership directly above their rank. If the member is concerned with a leader directly above their rank, they may ask a higher leader to remediate their issue.

7.

If a member misspells TOG when changing their gamertag (example: Tog, tog, etc.) the member will not be permitted to move into a staff position until the name is corrected.

8.

Members must inform squad leaders beforehand if possible and the reason why they could not attend the amount of meeting/game-nights they are required to attend.

9.

Members who have personal issues of any kind and needs to go inactive must inform the leadership above them and ask for a LOA (leave of absence) every member within TOG if requesting/needing a LOA will talk to his/her general and then the general can decide to okay the LOA.

10.

Members are not permitted to be a part of any other clan, community or organization this also includes members that have other Xbox Live Gamertags.

11.

Members must maintain a professional manner and attitude during play, lobby chat, party chat and any other TOG-based chats in order to represent TOG as a positive gaming community.

12.

All community members must read and understand the ranking structure listed at community-ranks.


We have a 3 strike action tolerance in the community

•First offense is a warning.
  •Second offense is a suspension.
    •Third offense execute order 66.


Squad/Division Transfer Policy

A transfer could be needed for any number of reasons, from dating to losing a game share. For whatever reason, a Division Transfer must be approved by both Division Leaders and both Generals if it pertains to them. If it is a Squad Transfer the Division Leader and both Generals must agree. The transferring member must also find one new recruit for the squad he/she is leaving and find two recruits for the squad he/she is entering.


Dating Policy

We understand that it wouldn't be out of the ordinary for members to grow feelings for each other with males and females working together under such close conditions. But alas, we must strongly advise against this, as if a breakup were to happen, synergy would be lost, and far too many resources would be dedicated to solving any problems that may arise following said breakup. To prevent any setbacks and to ensure enjoyment of all members, we must ask for members to avoid dating someone from the same squad. If dating occurs within the same squad, a transfer is mandatory. If transferred, it is possible that the position the perpetrator once held is already taken. Therefore it is possible for a demotion to occur. Please remember that TOG is a Gaming Community, not a Dating Website.


Sexual Harassment Policy

As leaders, role models, and human beings, we understand how much damage sexual harassment can cause to ones psyche. For this reason any and all sexual harassment cases are of the utmost importance. But before a claim is made, the perpetrator must be blocked. If harassment continues, a squad leader must be presented with substantial evidence (such as screenshots, voice recording with visible representation, screen recordings, etc…) This is to ensure the safety of all parties involved. If found guilty the member will immediately have their membership revoked.


D-dosing Policy

Any action that involes you taking people offline, taking someone's account, or recording personal info for a nefarious purpose will not be tolerated and will result in immediate termination of your member ship in TOG if you're found guilty by the security committee.


If you do not read this rule book your head will be shaved and you will deal with the dad of TOG.

Code of conduct may change as progression follows.
